Lee Kuan Yew

Singapore's Founding Father

1923 - 2015

Biography

Lee Kuan Yew was a Singaporean statesman and lawyer who served as the first Prime Minister of Singapore from 1959 to 1990. He is widely recognized as the founding father of modern Singapore.

Born into a middle-class family during the British colonial era, Lee was educated at Raffles Institution and later at the University of Cambridge, where he studied law. Upon returning to Singapore, he became involved in politics and co-founded the People's Action Party (PAP) in 1954.

Under his leadership, Singapore transformed from a developing country to a developed nation within a single generation, achieving remarkable economic growth and social stability. His pragmatic approach to governance and emphasis on meritocracy, multiracialism, and economic development became known as the "Singapore Model."

Lee Kuan Yew's legacy continues to influence Singapore's policies and governance philosophy today, making him one of the most influential political figures in Southeast Asian history.
